The History of Yellowstone

Yellowstone National Park, established in 1872, is the first national park in the world.

Spanning over 2.2 million acres, the park sits on top of a volcanic hotspot and is home to geothermal wonders like the famous Old Faithful geyser and the stunning Grand Prismatic Spring. The park’s diverse ecosystems range from lush forests to vast meadows, supporting wildlife such as bison, elk, bears, and wolves.

Yellowstone's cultural significance dates back thousands of years, as multiple Native American tribes have called this area home. The park’s protected landscapes have provided countless opportunities for discovery, education, and recreation for visitors worldwide.

Learn more about the park’s breathtaking landscapes, fascinating wildlife, and incredible geothermal features by visiting Yellowstone History & Culture.
